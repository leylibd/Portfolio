Interactive Cubes

Introduction

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a life-long neurodivergent disorder. The main characteristics often are:


  • communication problems;
  • limited motor skills;
  • difficulties in reading emotions;
  • attention and focus problems;
  • difficulties in relating to people, things and events;
  • difficulties in interacting with people;
  • repetitive body motions and behaviour like repeating sounds or phrases;

Many children with ASD require an environment that is calm, with clear and familiar structures, and learning activities that can be customized to their needs [1].


Literature review

During the last few decades, computer technologies have been used as a supporting tool to enhance learning and motivation of kids with ASD. Prior research shows that usage of technologies has a positive effect on kids with ASD. ‘Parents and clinicians regularly report that children with autism are drawn to technological devices’ (Goldsmith & LeBlanc, 2004: 166). Multiple studies state that kids with autism often have unusual interest in sensory interactions: compared to verbal output and rewards, sensory rewards provide more positive reaction and result in significant increase in verbal initiations [2, 9]. Other studies have found that instructions that were given through computers have positive affect on kids with ASD and result in increased motivation, attention and learning and decreased inappropriate behaviour [2]. Moreover, integration of tangible user interfaces into behavioral therapies resulted in “positive effects for simulating attention, shortening duration of solitary activities and lowering arousal” (Zhiglova, 2018).


Lahm (1996) conducted an initial investigation into the impact of software features in commercially available programs on engagement, affect, and choice. 48 alternating treatment design studies found that children with disabilities like autism prefer programs with more interactive elements, such as animation, sound, and voice. These programs resulted in more positive emotions and more frequent engagement with the computer. The children were also observed to make choices based on their preferences for these interactive features [2, 3].

Range of activities with multi-sensory feedback help to enhance attention and social skills and promote the interaction between caregiver and a child [6].


Project goal

My goal is to create an interactive digital system for children with autism that:


  • can help them to improve basic life skills like cognitive, social and motor in a home setting through interactive activities;
  • is based on tactile and tangible interaction;
  • doesn’t need a professional assistance;
  • enhances interaction between parents and kids;
  • is customised to each child’s case and needs

Conceptual Model

Interactive Cubes is an interactive system that helps children between the age of 5-10 with low functioning Autism Spectrum Disorder develop their motor, cognitive and social skills together with their parents. It comes with a mobile app where parents can select different activities and tasks for their child.


An AI that is integrated in the app and trained by professional psychologists, will tailor activities to meet individual needs of the child. It will set goals and track the process. After each activity, parent can complete a quiz on what they noticed in child's behaviour during the activity. They can also write down their observations so AI can learn more about a child and consider these things in building upcoming activities. Before using the app, parents will need to answer a set of questions about the child’s state so that AI can tailor the activities according to it.

However, this system is not meant to replace actual applied behavioral therapy. Rather, it can be used as a tool that makes it easier for parents to spend time with their children by learning and playing.

Description of the cubes

Each cube has a electrostatic sensory display on top, where the images for activities will be displayed. The display will also be used to explore different textures through tactile sensation. Other components of the cube are speakers and microphone. The cube is hollow inside.


Interaction

Before the start of the activities, a parent will open each cube that will be involved in the task-based activities and put a small reward inside. It can be whatever child likes, for example, their favorite candy, toy, etc.
Parent selects an activity in the app, for example, image recognition. An app shows a certain image and parent tells them to find a cube with this it.
**Right answer:**
If a child touches the right cube, one side of this cube will open and they will get a small reward, praising them for a correct answer and thereby creating a positive reinforcement. Example video of the prototype: /////PASTE VIDEO
**Wrong answer:**
If a child touches a wrong cube, cube will vibrate and won’t give a reward (aversive stimulus).
